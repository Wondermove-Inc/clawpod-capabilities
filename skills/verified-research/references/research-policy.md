# Research policy

## Source hierarchy

1. Contemporaneous primary records and authoritative datasets.
2. Peer-reviewed or institutionally reviewed analysis with inspectable methods.
3. Reputable reporting with named sources and corrections practice.
4. Specialist commentary with disclosed evidence.
5. Aggregators, social posts, and anonymous claims only as leads, never sole support for consequential facts.

Independence means sources do not merely repeat the same upstream claim. Recency matters when facts can change; historical claims may favor contemporaneous records. Explain material selection limits.

## Contradictions and confidence

Preserve each conflicting claim and its provenance. Check definitions, dates, scope, revisions, and primary records. Mark unresolved conflicts explicitly. High confidence requires direct, authoritative, internally consistent evidence; medium allows modest limitations; low means indirect, single-source, stale, or materially disputed evidence. Confidence is judgment owned by the agent, not the Harness.
